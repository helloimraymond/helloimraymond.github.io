Seeds of Life: Mapping Sperm Count Across Countries

Target Audience: Educated lay audience Tools: Excel, Illustrator, Photoshop, Blender Client: Shehryar Saharan (BMC Faculty)

This multivariate data visualization examines sperm counts, fertility rates, and other related metrics across select countries, incorporating various factors known to affect male reproductive health. The sperm, each representing a country, swim towards the egg in a captivating metaphor, illustrating the many environmental and societal influences that shape global reproductive health.

Research & Data Analysis.

To begin, I browsed academic papers and online databases to gather data on sperm count, fertility, and other related healthcare factors. I organized the collected data in a spreadsheet and created initial graphs using Excel to better understand the graphical trends, which later served as a foundation for the infographic's final layout, which I began to plan in this stage with quick thumbnail sketches.

Layout & Graphic Design.

I produced a series of thumbnails to explore visual metaphors and potential layouts. From these, I developed a basic layout for the infographic, placing particular emphasis on establishing a clear narrative flow.

Using Illustrator, I transferred the raw graphs and began refining the layout, incorporating colour, typography, and visual hierarchy to improve clarity. I also modelled a human egg in Blender to serve as the focal point of the metaphor.
